about jableh

Jableh has been inhabited since at least the second millennium BCE. The city was part of the Ugaritic kingdom and was mentioned as "Gbʿly" in the archives of the city c. 1200 BC.In antiquity Jableh (then called Gabala) was an important Hellenistic and then Roman city. One of the main remains of this period is a theatre, capable of housing c. 7,000 spectators. In the medieval period, Jableh, then called Gibellum, was conquered by Christian crusaders on 23 July 1109 and was part of a Crusader State until it was captured by Saladin in 1189 during the Third Crusade.

Zaytoun restaurant

Zaytoun Tourist Complex is one of the ancient landmarks in the city of Jableh. It has been restored and now has a restaurant and a café, in addition to a swimming pool. It is distinguished by its location on the seafront. It also has a place designated for children’s activities.

Dawn Tawn restaurant

Downtown is considered one of the modern restaurants in Jableh. It overlooks the seafront and features a wonderful terrace, in addition to its delicious food and modern design.

Moualla restaurant

Maualla is distinguished by many things. It is also located on the seafront. It includes a restaurant and a café, in addition to a sports club, which is one of the most beautiful and modern sports clubs.
